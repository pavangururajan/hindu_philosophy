# 4-Year Hindu Philosophy Curriculum: From Foundations to Madhwa Tattvavada

**Student:** Pavan
**Pace:** Medium (~1-2 hours per class, 1 class per week)
**Goal:** A systematic, progressive study of Hindu philosophy, starting with broad foundations and culminating in a deep dive into Madhvacharya's Dvaita Vedanta (Tattvavada).
**Format:** Each class includes Devanagari, IAST transliteration, English translation, word-by-word breakdown for key shlokas, and a gradual Sanskrit vocabulary track.

---

## Year 1: The Foundations of Hindu Thought
*Objective: Understand the core concepts, the structure of the scriptures, and the basic worldview of Sanatana Dharma.*

**Semester 1: The Architecture of the Scriptures & Core Concepts**
* Class 1.1: Introduction to the Vedas, Shruti vs. Smriti, and the Prasthanatrayi
* Class 1.2: The Concepts of Brahman, Atman, and Jagat (World)
* Class 1.3: The Mechanics of Karma, Samsara, and Moksha
* Class 1.4: Dharma: Cosmic Order and Individual Duty
* Class 1.5: The Four Purusharthas (Goals of Life) and Ashramas (Stages of Life)

**Semester 2: Introduction to the Upanishads (The Principal Texts)**
* Class 1.6: Isha Upanishad: Action and Renunciation
* Class 1.7: Kena Upanishad: The Power behind the Senses
* Class 1.8: Katha Upanishad (Part 1): Nachiketa and Yama (The Nature of Death)
* Class 1.9: Katha Upanishad (Part 2): The Chariot Analogy and the Self
* Class 1.10: Taittiriya Upanishad: The Five Sheaths (Pancha Kosha)
* Class 1.11: Mandukya Upanishad: The Four States of Consciousness and OM

**Semester 3: The Bhagavad Gita (The Essence of the Upanishads)**
* Class 1.12: Gita Ch 1-2: The Crisis of Arjuna and Sankhya Yoga (The Immortal Self)
* Class 1.13: Gita Ch 3-4: Karma Yoga (Action without Attachment)
* Class 1.14: Gita Ch 5-6: Sannyasa and Dhyana Yoga (Meditation)
* Class 1.15: Gita Ch 7-9: Vijnana and Raja Vidya (Knowledge of the Supreme)
* Class 1.16: Gita Ch 10-11: Vibhuti and Vishwaroopa (The Cosmic Form)
* Class 1.17: Gita Ch 12-15: Bhakti Yoga and the Supreme Person (Purushottama)
* Class 1.18: Gita Ch 16-18: The Three Gunas and Final Surrender

---

## Year 2: The Six Darshanas (Orthodox Schools of Philosophy)
*Objective: Explore the six classical systems of Indian philosophy to understand how different thinkers approached epistemology, logic, and metaphysics.*

**Semester 1: Logic, Nature, and Practice**
* Class 2.1: Introduction to the Shad-Darshanas and Pramanas (Sources of Knowledge)
* Class 2.2: Nyaya Darshana: The School of Logic and Epistemology
* Class 2.3: Vaisheshika Darshana: Atomism and the Categories of Reality
* Class 2.4: Samkhya Darshana: Purusha (Consciousness) and Prakriti (Matter)
* Class 2.5: Yoga Darshana (Patanjali): The Eight Limbs (Ashtanga) and Mind Control

**Semester 2: Ritual and the Culmination of Knowledge**
* Class 2.6: Purva Mimamsa: The Philosophy of Vedic Rituals and Dharma
* Class 2.7: Uttara Mimamsa (Vedanta): Introduction to the Brahma Sutras
* Class 2.8: The Evolution of Vedanta: How one text led to multiple interpretations

---

## Year 3: Comparative Vedanta (Advaita, Vishishtadvaita, Dvaita)
*Objective: Understand the three major schools of Vedanta side-by-side. This comparative approach is crucial for appreciating the specific arguments Madhvacharya makes later.*

**Semester 1: Advaita (Non-Dualism) - Adi Shankaracharya**
* Class 3.1: The Core Tenets of Advaita: Brahman, Maya, and Jiva
* Class 3.2: The Concept of Mithya (Illusion) and the Two Levels of Reality
* Class 3.3: Advaita's Interpretation of "Tat Tvam Asi" (Thou Art That)

**Semester 2: Vishishtadvaita (Qualified Non-Dualism) - Ramanujacharya**
* Class 3.4: The Core Tenets of Vishishtadvaita: Chit, Achit, and Ishvara
* Class 3.5: The Body-Soul Relationship (Sharira-Shariri Bhava)
* Class 3.6: The Role of Prapatti (Surrender) and Bhakti

**Semester 3: Introduction to Dvaita (Dualism) - Madhvacharya**
* Class 3.7: The Life and Mission of Sri Madhvacharya (Ananda Tirtha)
* Class 3.8: The Core Tenets of Dvaita: Hari Sarvottamatva (Supremacy of Vishnu)
* Class 3.9: The Pancha Bheda (The Five Fundamental Differences)
* Class 3.10: Vayu Jeevottamatva: The Role of Vayu/Mukhyaprana

---

## Year 4: Deep Dive into Madhwa Tattvavada (Dvaita Vedanta)
*Objective: A rigorous study of Madhvacharya's philosophy through his Sarvamoola Granthas and key Prakarana Granthas.*

**Semester 1: Epistemology and the Nature of Reality**
* Class 4.1: Pramana Lakshana: Madhva's Theory of Knowledge (Pratyaksha, Anumana, Agama)
* Class 4.2: Tattva Sankhyana & Tattva Viveka: The Categories of Reality (Svatantra vs. Paratantra)
* Class 4.3: The Concept of Vishesha: How substance and attributes relate

**Semester 2: The Supremacy of Vishnu (Vishnu Tattva Nirnaya)**
* Class 4.4: Vishnu Tattva Nirnaya (Part 1): The Apaurusheyatva of the Vedas
* Class 4.5: Vishnu Tattva Nirnaya (Part 2): Refutation of Advaita (Maya and Mithya)
* Class 4.6: Vishnu Tattva Nirnaya (Part 3): Establishing Narayana as the Supreme Brahman

**Semester 3: Madhva's Prasthanatrayi Bhashya**
* Class 4.7: Gita Bhashya & Gita Tatparya: Madhva's unique take on the Bhagavad Gita
* Class 4.8: Upanishad Bhashyas: Reinterpreting the Mahavakyas (e.g., "Atat Tvam Asi")
* Class 4.9: Brahma Sutra Bhashya & Anuvyakhyana: Defending Dualism logically

**Semester 4: Devotion, Grace, and Liberation**
* Class 4.10: The Concept of Bimba-Pratibimba Bhava (The Soul as a Reflection)
* Class 4.11: Taratamya: The Hierarchy of Souls
* Class 4.12: Swarupananda: The Nature of Moksha in Dvaita
* Class 4.13: The Role of Aparoksha Jnana and Prasad (Divine Grace)
* Class 4.14: Conclusion: Living the Tattvavada Philosophy Today
