# Cumulative Sanskrit Glossary

This document contains a growing list of Sanskrit terms encountered during the 4-Year Hindu Philosophy Curriculum. Terms are added progressively as each class is completed.

| Sanskrit Term (IAST) | Devanagari | English Meaning | Context / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Shruti | श्रुति | "That which is heard" | The revealed, unauthored scriptures (Vedas). |
| Smriti | स्मृति | "That which is remembered" | Authored texts based on tradition (Epics, Puranas). |
| Veda | वेद | "Knowledge" | The four foundational texts of Hinduism. |
| Upanishad | उपनिषद् | "Sitting near" | The philosophical portions of the Vedas; the foundation of Vedanta. |
| Prasthanatrayi | प्रस्थानत्रयी | "The three starting points" | The three foundational texts of Vedanta (Upanishads, Brahma Sutras, Bhagavad Gita). |
| Apaurusheya | अपौरुषेय | "Not of human origin" | A key characteristic of the Vedas. |
